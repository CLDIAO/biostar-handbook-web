{% extends "unitbase.html" %} {% load pytags %} {# title = Troubles in your PATH #} {# subtitle = when good PATHs go bad #} {# name = What to do when you accidentally nuke your PATH #} {% block body %} {% markdown %} If you mistype the `PATH` variable while adding it into the `.bash_profile` you may end up with the unenviable situation of applying an incorrect setting every time your terminal initializes. For example on a beautiful morning you'll do a: echo 'export PATH=~/bin:PATH' >> ~/.bash_profile The net effect of this is that even basic system programs that used to work won't do so anymore: $ ls -bash: ls: command not found Congratulations! You've just nuked your `PATH`. You forgot the `$` sign on the right hand side of the `PATH` variable. Instead of substituting the new `PATH` with the value of `$PATH` you are setting it to be the word `PATH`. Not so good. Your command should have looked like this: echo 'export PATH=~/bin:$PATH' >> ~/.bash_profile Oops! - - - ### Explanation Jules in Pulp Fiction explains it best: > The `PATH` to any righteous tool is beset on all sides by the > inequities of unix and the tyranny of environment variables. > > Blessed is he who, in the name of charity and good will, shepherds the weak > through the valley of darkness, for he is truly his brother's keeper > and the finder of lost programs. Good news: there is probably no command line guru that did not do this at one point in their lives. So you are well on your way to guru-ness. - - - ### How to fix it Open a terminal. Oh yes, it is still broken. Fix the `PATH` within the terminal. export PATH=/bin:/usr/bin Edit your `.bash_profile` with `nano` and remove the lines that cause the trouble: nano ~/.bash_profile `nano` is very quirky. Take it as punishment meted out for clobbering your path. It could be worse. At least your don't have to use `vi`. Edit the file, correct the offending line(s) then save the file with `crtl + O` (that is capital letter O) then exit the editor. Open a NEW terminal window and verify that your "corrections" make sense. Rinse and repeat until it works. **Good luck comrade!** - - - Shortcuts to other installation related information: * {% link "computer-setup" %} - hardware setup. * {% link "how-to-install-everything" %} - to install every tool in this book. * {% link "bash-profile.html" %} - to ensure that your settings are applied automatically. {% endmarkdown %} {% endblock %}
